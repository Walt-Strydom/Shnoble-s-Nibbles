Dog Health & Tips

A happy dog starts with good daily habits — and a treat they can actually trust. Here's what we believe in.

Daily habits for a healthy dog

Hydration First

Keep cool, clean water available all day — especially in South Africa's warm weather. Freeze-dried treats are low in moisture, so water access is especially important.

Treat Portions Matter

Treats should make up no more than 8% of your dog's daily calorie intake. Even with the healthiest treats, moderation keeps weight and digestion in check.

Train with Purpose

Use small, high-value treats like ours to reinforce good behaviour. Short sessions of 5–10 minutes work best — dogs learn fastest when they're engaged, not bored.

Read the Label

The first ingredient listed is the most prominent. If it's a filler or "meat by-product", put it back. Real meat or vegetables should lead the list — see how we do it.

Play Every Day

Daily physical and mental stimulation helps dogs stay fit, calm, and mentally sharp. A well-exercised dog is a happy dog — and a better treat receiver.

Vet Check-Ups

Regular vet visits catch issues early. Always consult your vet before making significant changes to your dog's diet, especially for puppies, seniors, or dogs with conditions.

Important: Shnoble's Nibbles treats are designed as a supplement to a balanced diet, not a replacement. Always ensure your dog has access to fresh water when enjoying our freeze-dried treats.

Common questions from SA dog parents

Quick answers to the questions we hear most often — covering treats, nutrition, and daily care.

How many treats should I give my dog per day? +

Treats should make up no more than 8% of your dog's daily calorie intake. For a 10 kg dog eating roughly 600 calories per day, that's about 48 calories from treats. With freeze-dried treats like ours, a small handful is usually sufficient — they're nutrient-dense, so a little goes a long way.

What should I look for on a dog treat label? +

Check that the first ingredient is a named protein (like chicken liver or beef) rather than a filler like corn, wheat, or soy. Avoid treats with artificial colours, artificial flavours, xylitol, propylene glycol, or vague terms like "meat by-products". See exactly what's in Shnoble's treats →

Are freeze-dried dog treats safe? +

Yes. Freeze-drying removes moisture at low temperatures, preserving nutrients without the need for preservatives or heat treatment. Always ensure your dog has access to fresh water when eating freeze-dried treats, as they are very low in moisture content.

How much water does my dog need per day? +

A general guide is 50–60 ml of water per kg of body weight per day. For a 10 kg dog, that's around 500–600 ml daily. Fresh, clean water should always be freely available — especially important if your dog eats freeze-dried treats, which contain very little moisture.
