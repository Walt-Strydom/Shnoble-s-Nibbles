Your ingredient questions — answered

We believe an informed pet parent is the best pet parent. Here are the questions we hear most often.

Is chicken liver good for dogs? +

Yes — cooked chicken liver is one of the most nutrient-dense foods you can offer your dog. It's rich in protein, vitamins A and B12, iron, copper, zinc, and omega fatty acids. We use it as the primary ingredient in every Shnoble's Nibbles treat for exactly that reason. Note: as with all organ meats, moderation is key — perfect as a daily treat rather than a meal replacement.

Can dogs eat sweet potato? +

Absolutely. Cooked sweet potato is safe, nutritious, and highly digestible for dogs. It provides dietary fibre, vitamins A, C, and B-complex vitamins, as well as potassium and beta-carotene antioxidants. Raw sweet potato should be avoided (can cause digestive upset), but cooked sweet potato is an excellent complement to protein in a balanced treat.

What is bone broth for dogs? +

Bone broth is made by slow-simmering bones to extract collagen, glucosamine, chondroitin, and minerals. For dogs, it supports joint health and mobility, aids gut health, and adds a deeply irresistible aroma and flavour. We use beef bone broth — no onion, no salt, no additives — as the third ingredient in our treats.

Are freeze-dried treats better than baked treats? +

Freeze-drying and baking are both valid methods, but freeze-drying preserves more of the original nutritional content. High baking temperatures can degrade heat-sensitive vitamins and enzymes. Freeze-drying removes moisture at low temperatures, locking in nutrients without needing preservatives. The result is a shelf-stable, nutrient-rich treat with a texture dogs love.
